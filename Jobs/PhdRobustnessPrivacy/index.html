Research topic - Robustness and privacy in P2P

Fully distributed based P2P systems have no single trusted server or administrative domain. Given this, how can a P2P system be robust against malicious attacks (peers that enter the system purely to disrupt it) and protect well behaved peers privacy. For example, in a filesharing system, or a distributed social networking system, how can peers protect themselves from others who wish to track their location and activities? What kinds of approaches can provide robustness against "Sybil" and other similar kinds of attacks.

What kinds of fully distributed approaches could be applied to improve robustness and privacy in P2P applications?
Possible approaches may include (though are not limited to):

  • Attack resistant P2P gossiping protocols
  • Distributed P2P social networking that protects peer privacy
  • Social / peer obfuscation methods
  • Distributed policing / monitoring methods
  • "Democratic de-cloaking" - mechanisms that allow for a member of a community to lose their privacy and / or be excluded if the majority of trusted peers decide to do so. Think "name and shame" or Athenian Greek "Ostracism" ​http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ostracism.
