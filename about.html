About Tribler

Tribler is a research project at Delft University of Technology and Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam.Tribler was created six years ago as a new open source Peer-to-Peer file sharing program. During this time over one million users have used it, and three generations of Ph.D. students tested their algorithms in the real world.

Work on Tribler was initiated in 2005 and has been supported by multiple European grants.

Tribler is the first client which continuously tries to improve upon the basic BitTorrent implementation by addressing some of its flaws. It implements, amongst others, remote search, streaming, channels and reputation-management. All these features are implemented in a completely distributed manner, not relying on any centralized component. Still, Tribler manages to remain fully backwards compatible with BitTorrent.


Tribler has been Internet deployed for 7 years now. Roughly 10 to 15 scientists and engineers work on it full-time Our ambition is to make darknet technology, security and privacy the default. As of 2013 we have received code from 46 contributors and 143.705 lines of code.


Vision & Mission

"Push the boundaries of self-organising systems, robust reputation systems and craft collaborative systems with millions of active participants under continuous attack from spammers and other adversarial entities."
